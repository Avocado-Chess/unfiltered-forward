Origin Story

How It Started

Unfiltered Forward was born from a simple, frustrating truth: that in the digital age, the ability to speak freely is not guaranteed — not by the platforms we use every day, and not even by the governments meant to protect us.

It started with two teenagers who noticed it. Scrolling through feeds, watching posts disappear, accounts silenced, ideas buried by algorithms with no explanation. They asked the question that adults had stopped asking: why does this feel so wrong?

"We are young enough to still believe that things can change — and old enough to do something about it."

In 2025, Bhanusri Durbhakula and Leyla Bibb founded Unfiltered Forward with a conviction that free speech on social media isn't a privilege — it's a right worth fighting for. Their goal: to raise awareness about digital censorship and to put forward real, actionable policy ideas that protect every voice, everywhere.

We are just getting started. And we are not staying quiet.

Our Mission

To expose and combat digital censorship, advocate for robust free speech protections on social media platforms, and develop forward-thinking policy proposals that hold governments and big tech accountable to the people they serve.

New Policy Ideas

Bold Ideas.
Real Change.

Awareness alone isn't enough. Unfiltered Forward puts forward concrete, actionable policy proposals to protect free expression online — from the platforms that host us and the governments that govern us.

01
Transparency & Accountability
The Platform Transparency Act

Social media companies make millions of content moderation decisions every day — and users almost never find out why their post was removed, their account restricted, or their reach quietly throttled. That has to change.

We propose requiring all major platforms to publish clear, accessible data on censorship actions — and to give users real explanations when their content is removed.

Mandatory quarterly "Censorship Transparency Reports" disclosing total content removals, categories, and appeal outcomes.
Require platforms to notify users with a specific reason within 48 hours of any content removal or account restriction.
Create an independent oversight board with public representation to audit platform moderation data annually.
Establish a public, searchable database of platform content policies so users know the rules before they post.
+
02
Algorithmic Rights
The Anti-Suppression Act

Platforms don't just delete content — they quietly bury it. Shadow banning, reduced reach, and algorithmic down-ranking can silence voices just as effectively as outright removal, with no notice and no recourse. This invisible censorship must be addressed.

Ban undisclosed shadow banning — if a platform reduces a user's reach, the user must be informed.
Require platforms to offer a fully chronological, algorithm-free feed as a default user option.
Give users the legal right to request an explanation for any significant, unexplained drop in reach or engagement.
Prohibit algorithmic suppression based on political viewpoint, religion, or protected identity characteristics.
+
03
Content Moderation Reform
The Viewpoint Neutrality Standard

Content moderation must be consistent, fair, and blind to political leaning. When platforms apply rules differently based on who is speaking — rather than what is being said — they stop being neutral platforms and start being political actors. We propose a neutrality standard with teeth.

Require platforms to apply content moderation rules consistently across all political viewpoints with regular audits to verify compliance.
Create an independent appeals process where users can challenge politically motivated removals before a neutral third party.
Establish civil penalties for platforms that demonstrate a pattern of viewpoint-based discrimination in moderation decisions.
Mandate that AI moderation tools be tested for political bias before deployment and results published publicly.
